COCKTAIL
/ˈkäkˌtāl/

noun

noun: cocktail; plural noun: cocktails

1. an alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit or several spirits mixed with other ingredients, such as fruit juice, lemonade, or cream.

a mixture of substances or factors, especially when dangerous or unpleasant in its effects.

"financial pressure plus isolation can be a deadly cocktail for some people"

2. a dish consisting of small pieces of food, typically served cold at the beginning of a meal as an appetizer.

"a chilled lobster cocktail prettily presented in a martini glass"
